Pace Zones

Assign runs to any days you want. Keep at least one easy/rest day between the Pace run and the Long Run. On hot or hilly days, go by effort, not pace.

ZonePaceFeel
Easy9:30 – 10:15/miConversational, nose-breathing
Long Run9:15 – 9:45/miEasy but steady
Pace (Higdon)8:15 – 8:35/miMarathon goal effort — comfortably hard
Tempo7:50 – 8:10/miHarder than pace, sustainable 20-40 min
Interval7:15 – 7:40/miHard, 800m–1mi reps with jog recovery

How This Plan Works

  • 4 runs per week. Higdon programs 3 runs + cross-training. This plan adds a 4th easy run. If you can only do 3, drop Run 3 (the short easy) — never skip the long run.
  • Days are flexible. Runs are labeled 1–4. Slot them into any days. Just separate the Pace run and Long Run by at least one rest/easy day.
  • Stepback weeks (marked with a gray border) reduce mileage every 3rd week. These are built into Higdon's plan — don't skip them, they prevent overtraining.
  • Cross-training: Higdon prescribes cross-training 1x/week. Your push/pull gym days count. Scale back leg day intensity once weekly miles pass 30.
  • Missed a run? Don't double up. Continue with the next run. Consistency beats perfection.
  • Injury check: If shin/knee/ankle pain appears during a run, cut it short. If it persists across 2+ runs, take 3–4 days fully off.
  • Shoes: If your current pair causes ankle pain, get fitted at a running store before Week 3.

Race Day Strategy

  • Miles 1–6: Start at 8:50–9:00/mi. You will feel amazing. Do not speed up. Bank patience, not time.
  • Miles 7–13: Settle into 8:30–8:45. Take a gel at mile 7 and every 45 min after.
  • Miles 14–20: This is the real race. Hold 8:20–8:40. Stay mentally engaged, one mile at a time.
  • Miles 21–26.2: Everything you have left. If you went out conservative, you’ll have legs here. Push toward 8:00 if you can.
  • SF hills: Don’t fight uphills — shorten stride, maintain effort (not pace). Recover on downhills.
  • Fueling: Gel every ~45 min. Water at every aid station. Don’t try anything new on race day.

Nutrition & Recovery

  • Long run fueling: Start practicing gels/chews by Week 6. Take one every 45 min during runs over 13 mi.
  • Daily protein: At 170 lbs, aim for 130–150g protein daily to support both running and lifting.
  • Carb loading: 3 days before race, increase carbs to ~3.5g per lb bodyweight (~600g/day).
  • Sleep: 7–8 hours minimum during peak weeks. Sleep is where adaptation happens.
  • Foam roll / stretch: 10 min after every run, especially calves, quads, IT band.
