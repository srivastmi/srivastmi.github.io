Pace Zones

All paces are flexible — use perceived effort on hilly or hot days. Assign runs to any days you want; just keep at least one easy/rest day between the Quality session and the Long Run.

How This Plan Works

  • 4 runs per week. If you can only do 3, drop the optional easy run — never skip the long run.
  • Days are flexible. The runs are labeled Run 1–4. Slot them into any days that work. Just separate hard efforts by at least one easy/rest day.
  • Strength training: Keep push/pull. Scale back leg day intensity as weekly miles climb past 30 — shift to bodyweight/mobility work for legs.
  • Missed a run? Don't double up. Just continue with the next scheduled run. Consistency over perfection.
  • Shin/knee/ankle check: If pain appears during a run, cut it short. Ice, rest, resume when pain-free. Shin splints recurrence = take 3 days off running entirely.
  • Shoe note: If your current shoes cause ankle pain, get fitted at a running store before week 3. You need reliable shoes for 16 weeks.

Race Day Strategy

  • Miles 1–6: Start at 8:40–8:50/mi. You will feel amazing. Do not speed up. Bank patience, not time.
  • Miles 7–13: Settle into 8:20–8:35. Take a gel at mile 7 and every 5 miles after.
  • Miles 14–20: This is the real race. Hold 8:15–8:35. Stay mentally engaged, one mile at a time.
  • Miles 21–26.2: Everything you have left. If you went out conservative, you'll have legs here. Push to 8:00 or under if you can.
  • SF hills: Don't fight the uphills — shorten stride, maintain effort (not pace). Recover on downhills.
  • Fueling: Gel every ~45 min. Water at every aid station. Don't try anything new on race day.

Nutrition & Recovery

  • Long run fueling: Start practicing gels/chews by week 7. Take one every 45 min during runs over 13 mi.
  • Daily protein: At 170 lbs, aim for 130–150g protein daily to support both running and lifting.
  • Carb loading: 3 days before race, increase carbs to ~3.5g per lb bodyweight (~600g/day).
  • Sleep: 7–8 hours minimum during peak weeks. Sleep is where adaptation happens.
  • Foam roll / stretch: 10 min after every run, especially calves, quads, IT band.
